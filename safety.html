FLOOD


- Climb to Higher Areas BEFORE Water Starts Rising
- Discuss Plans With Family and Designate Meeting Spot and Contact Person
- Evacuate Immediately if Needed. (Grab Emergency Flood Bag, Disconnect All Electronics, Move Valuable Items to Higher Levels, Seal Basement Vents, and Bring All Outdoor Possessions Indoors if Time Permits).
- Fill Car With Gas and Only Drive on SAFE route. DO NOT DRIVE OR SWIM ON FLOODED ROADS.
- If Someone Gets Trapped in the Flood, DO NOT GO BACK. Use Flotation Devices or Call 911

DROUGHTS

* To Avoid Drought, Techniques to Save Water Must be Occurring Before Drought Occurs. Below are Just More Extreme Actions that Can be Taken During Periods of Drought.

-Flush Less Frequently.
-Dispose of Items That Would Usually Go Into the Toilet, Into the Trash.
-Avoid Baths, and Take Short Showers Instead With Limited Water Usage.
-Donâ€™t Allow Water to Run When Not Needed (While Brushing Teeth, Shaving, Washing Face Etc.).
-Catch Extra Shower and Sink Water to Water Plants.
-Use Water-Saving Dishwashers/Washing Machines Only When Full
-Do Not Rinse Dishes Before Putting Them in the Dishwasher. USe Utensils to Scrape Large Food Chunks.
-Do Not Water the Lawn and Plants. Replace With Plants That Requires Less Water.
-If Necessary, Only Water Plants/Lawn During the Cooler Temperatures ( Early Morning, Late Evening).

EARTHQUAKES

If Indoor
-Drop to The Floor and Take Cover Under Sturdy Furniture and HOLD ON to the Furniture Until Shaking Stops.
- If no Furniture is Present, Crouch in the Corner of the Building With Face and Head Covered With Arms.
- Stay Away From GLASS, WINDOW, OUTSIDE DOORS, and Anything Else That Has the Potential to Fall.
- If in Bed, Stay and Cover Head With Pillow, Unless Underneath a Heavy Light Fixture That May Fall.
- Use Doorway for Protection as a Last Resort. Only Use if Strongly Supported and Load-bearing
- STAY INSIDE UNTIL SHAKING STOPS.
- DO NOT USE ELEVATORS.
If Outdoor
- STAY OUTDOORS UNTIL SHAKING STOPS.
- Stay Away From Building, Streetlights and Utility Wires
If in Moving Vehicle
- Pull Over When Safe, and Stay in the Vehicle Until Shaking has Stopped.
- Do Not Stop Near or Under Trees, Buildings, Overpasses and Utility Wires.
- Once Earthquake is Over, Avoid Driving on Roads, Bridges or Ramps.

THUNDERSTORMS

-Get Out of Water
-Constantly Monitor NOAA Weather Radio, Local Television, www.weather.gov, and any other available resource.
-Seek Shelter. Get into a Vehicle but Avoid All Metal Parts. If no Building or Vehicle is Present, stay Near a Dense Group of Trees or Shrubs.
-DO NOT STAY NEAR TALL ISOLATED OBJECTS. If No Shelter is Present, Kneel Down, Feet Close Together With Head Tucked Down and Hands Over Ears. Keep All People Spread Out and Not Touching.
-DO NOT LIE DOWN.
AVOID all Objects That Conduct Electricity (GRAPHITE AND METAL) Including, But Not Limited to PADDLES, TENT POLES, CAMP STOVES, POWER LINES, UMBRELLAS, ETC. ).
-If Lightning Strike Occurs, Apply First Aid (victims can be revived with CPR), and CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY.

TORNADOES

-If you are in a Building
-Constantly Monitor NOAA Weather Radio, Local Television, www.weather.gov, and any other available resource.
-Go to the Basement or Any Small Interior Ground Floor Room.
-If no Basement is Available, Protect by Staying Under Heavy, Sturdy Furniture.
-Stay away from Windows, Exterior Walls and Doors.
-DO NOT USE ELEVATORS

If Outside/Driving
-AVOID VEHICLES AND MOBILE HOMES.
-Seek Shelter Indoors.
-If no shelter is present, Lie in a Ditch distanced from vehicles and mobile homes. Beware of Flooding and be Prepared to Move.
-If Tornado is Near and No Time is Available to Seek Shelter, Leave the Vehicle and Take Cover in a Low-Lying Area.

Caution: DO NOT CHASE TORNADOES. Although Tornadoes May Appear Still, it is Likely Moving Towards you.

HURRICANES

-Constantly Monitor NOAA Weather Radio, Local Television, www.weather.gov, and any other available resource.
-Stay Indoors and Avoid Windows, Skylights, and Glass Door. Stay in Interior Rooms, Closets or Innermost Downstairs Bathrooms.
-CLOSE ALL INTERIOR DOORS. SECURE ALL EXTERNAL DOORS.
-DO NOT LEAVE THE BUILDING UNTIL THERE IS CONFIRMATION THAT THE HURRICANE IS OVER.
-Lie on the Ground Under Sturdy Furniture.

Caution: Hurricanes can appear to be over, when in reality, you are at the eye of the storm. DO NOT LEAVE SAFE LOCATION UNTIL IT IS CONFIRMED THAT THE HURRICANE IS OVER

VOLCANOES


-Follow any Evacuation Orders From Authorities.
-Remain Indoors With Doors, Windows and Ventilation Closed Until Ash Settles.
-Constantly Monitor Radio and Local Television and Other Available Resources for Updates
-Change into Long Shirt and Pants With Sturdy Shoes. Wear Goggles or Glasses if Available. Wear a Mask to Filter Bad Air.
-Avoid Driving at All Costs. If You Must Drive, Drive Slowly and With Caution at 35 MPH or Slower.

FIRES

If There is a Fire in a House or Building:
-Locate the Nearest Fire Extinguisher and Use it Safely.
-Yell Fire Several Times and Alert Everyone in the Building That There is Fire. Get Out of the Building Quickly and Call 911.
-If Closed Doors/Handles are warm, DO NOT OPEN THE DOOR and locate another escape route.
-If Smoke, Heat or Flames Block Exit Route, Stay in a Safe Room with Doors Closed. Place Wet Towels Under the Door, Call 911 and Wave Bright Colored Clothes/Items from Windows.
-If you Must Escape Through Smoke, Keep Low Under Smoke and Close Doors After Exiting.
-IF YOUR CLOTHES CATCH ON FIRE, STOP MOVING, DROP TO THE GROUND WITH FACE COVERED, AND ROLL BACK AND FORTH UNTIL FLAMES GO OUT. DO NOT RUN.

If You are Caught in a Fire in Nature:
-Do Not Try to Outrun the Fire.
-Find a Body of Water (Lake, River, Etc.) to be in.
-If No Nearby Water is Present, Find a Cleared Area with Little Vegetation, Lie Low to the Ground, and Cover Body With Wet Clothing, Blanket or Soil.
-Protect Lungs by Breathing Air Close to the Ground (Under the Smoke ), Through Moist Cloth ( if Possible).
